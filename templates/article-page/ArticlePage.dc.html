Seven quiet habits of good sleepers

None of them involve buying anything. A look at the evening routines research keeps pointing back to — and how to borrow them without turning bedtime into a project.

K Kovitad Founder & curator · Updated June 2026

Ask a hundred good sleepers what they do before bed and you won't hear about gadgets. You'll hear about rhythm: the same rough bedtime, the same slow wind-down, most nights of the week. The research on sleep hygiene is unglamorous, and that's exactly why it works.

Light is the strongest lever. Dimming the house an hour before bed — and getting bright light within an hour of waking — does more for most people than any supplement marketed for sleep. It costs nothing and starts working within days.

Good sleep is less a skill and more a schedule you keep gently.

Temperature matters more than most people expect: a cool room and a warm shower beforehand nudge your core temperature in the direction sleep wants it to go. Research suggests moving the last coffee earlier is one of the highest-leverage changes a light sleeper can make.

A note on scope. This is general education and personal experience, not medical advice. If sleep problems persist, talk to your clinician — persistent insomnia and apnea deserve real care.

The rest of the seven are small: a notebook to park tomorrow's worries, a consistent wake time even after a bad night, keeping the bed for sleep, and treating the occasional rough night as weather — noticed, not fought.
